Instructions:


Welcome to Connector! You will be playing a word game with another player. You are the SPEAKER in this game, while the other player is the GUESSER. You will be providing clues to the the Guesser and the guesser will attempt to guess two words based of each clue.

Within a trial, as the Speaker, you will be presented with two words. Your task as the Speaker is to select any one-word clue for the two words and input that clue in the provided textbox as will be seen on the page. Please try your best to select clues that are related to both words. IF the clue entered is one of the two words provided then it will show that it is forbidden. For example, for the word-pair, “APPLE-MANGO”, FRUIT is a good clue because it is related to both APPLE and MANGO, whereas RED is a bad clue because it is only related to APPLE. Please ensure that you are always selecting a one-word clue, and that this clue is related to the words in meaning. Clues that give away the sound or letters of the words are not good clues. For example, for the word pair “CAT-BAT”, RHYME is not a good clue because it tells the Guesser to look for sound-related information instead of meaning-related information.

For any word pair, you will be able to type in one clue for each word pair and you will have 3 attempts to make the Guesser guess the correct words, after which the experiment will move on to the next word pair. You will be given 3 word pairs per trial, and 10 trials in total.

We will now have a practice session so that you are familiar with the game procedures. Click “Done” to continue.
