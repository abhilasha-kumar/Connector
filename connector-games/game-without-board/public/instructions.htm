Instructions:


Welcome to Connector! You will be playing a word game with another player. You are the GUESSER in this game, while the other player is the SPEAKER. You the guesser will be provided with a one word clue per trial to collectively identify certain words provided to the SPEAKER.

Within a trial, as the Guesser, you will be presented with a one-word clue which the Speaker has generated, and your task is to enter in the two input boxes below, the two words that the Speaker most likely intended to refer to, based on this clue. The Speaker has been instructed to choose one-word clues that are related to both words in meaning. For example, if the Speaker was given the word-pair “APPLE-MANGO”, they may come up with the clue “FRUIT”, which would be provided to you.

After entering these “candidates” or guesses, you should click “Done”.

For any word pair, you will have 3 attempts to choose the correct words, after which the experiment will move on to the next word pair. You will be guessing 3 word pairs per trial, and 10 trials in total.

We will now have a practice session so that you are familiar with the game procedures. Click “Done” to continue.
