Instructions:


Welcome to Connector! You will be playing a word game with another player. You are the SPEAKER in this game, while the other player is the GUESSER. You and the Guesser will both be looking at the same 20-item word board on a given trial and try to collectively identify certain words on the board.

Within a trial, as the Speaker, you will be presented with two words from the board. Your task as the Speaker is to select any one-word clue for the two words and input that clue in the provided textbox as will be seen on the page. Please try your best to select clues that are related to both words and are not words on the board. IF the clue entered is on the board then it will show that it is forbidden. For example, for the word-pair, “APPLE-MANGO”, FRUIT is a good clue because it is related to both APPLE and MANGO, whereas RED is a bad clue because it is only related to APPLE. Please ensure that you are always selecting one-word clues, and clues that are related to the words in meaning. Clues that give away the sound or letters of the words are not good clues. For example, for the word pair “CAT-BAT”, RHYME is not a good clue because it tells the Guesser to look for sound-related information instead of meaning-related information.



For any word pair, you will be able to type in one clue for each word pair and you will have 3 attempts to make the Guesser choose the correct words on the board, after which the experiment will move on to the next word pair. You will be given 3 word pairs per board, and 10 boards in total.

We will now have a practice session so that you are familiar with the game procedures. Click “Done” to continue.
